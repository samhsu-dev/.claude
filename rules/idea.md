# Research Idea Document

Paper-specific concept, hypothesis, and argumentation standards.

---

## Required Sections

### 1. Problem
- **Gap:** what is missing or broken in the state of the art (2-3 sentences).
- **Impact:** who is affected and what they cannot do today.
- **Evidence:** citations or data that prove the gap exists.

### 2. Thesis
- **Claim:** one sentence stating the proposed solution.
- **Insight:** the key observation that makes this approach viable.
- **Scope:** what this work covers and what it explicitly excludes.

### 3. Contributions
Numbered list. Each contribution:
- **What:** concrete artifact or finding.
- **How:** method or technique used.
- **Validation:** how correctness/value is demonstrated.

### 4. Approach Sketch
- High-level workflow or architecture (text or ASCII diagram).
- Key design decisions with rationale (why this over alternatives).
- Assumptions and prerequisites.

### 5. Evaluation Plan
- **Research Questions:** numbered RQs, each testable.
- **Metrics:** what is measured for each RQ.
- **Baselines:** comparison targets (prior tools, ablation, manual effort).
- **Dataset:** subject programs, benchmarks, or corpora.

### 6. Threats and Limitations
- Internal validity: confounds, bias, measurement error.
- External validity: generalizability boundaries.
- Known limitations acknowledged upfront.

---

## Rules

- Concepts and argumentation only. No LaTeX commands, no implementation details.
- Consistent terminology. Define a term once, use it everywhere.
- Every claim has evidence or a plan to obtain evidence.
- Audience: collaborators and reviewers. Write to convince, not to describe.
